// Overview

THE CONCEPT

LOGLINE

A man who travels between worlds appears as a guest on a podcast. The host thinks she's interviewing a quirky travel blogger. She has no idea he's passing through her reality on his way somewhere else.

THEME

GALAT TIME — Wrong Time. Being in the right place at the wrong moment. Or the wrong place at any moment. Time as something you survive, not manage.

// Character Bible

THE PLAYERS

THE HOST
The Performer
"The person who turned their entire personality into content."
She's the friend who screenshots everything for Instagram stories. She's the one who says "wait, say that again, I wasn't recording." She genuinely believes she's helping people by "creating content" and doesn't see the absurdity in turning every human moment into engagement metrics.
She's not stupid. She's just so deep in the content world that she's lost the ability to experience anything without framing it for an audience.
Energy
  • Always "on" — even when the camera isn't rolling, she behaves like it is
  • Upbeat but not manic — she's practiced this energy, it's smooth
  • Quick to laugh, quick to move on
  • Never sits in silence — fills every gap
  • Leans forward when she senses a "moment"
Speech Patterns
  • Speaks in podcast cadence — emphasis on certain words for effect
  • Uses phrases like "I love that," "That's so real," "Okay but like..."
  • Switches between English and Hindi fluidly (Hinglish)
  • Often interrupts, but warmly — she thinks she's helping
  • Has catchphrases she doesn't realize she repeats
  • Ends statements with upward inflection
What She Wants
  • A viral moment
  • To be seen as a thoughtful interviewer
  • To connect (genuinely, but she doesn't know how without performing)
  • Content. Always content.
What She Doesn't Realize
  • That she's not actually listening — she's waiting for her turn
  • That her guest is experiencing something she can't see
  • That her "deep moments" are surface-level
  • That her need to make everything "content" is a barrier to real connection
Physical Notes
  • Animated hands — gestures while speaking
  • Looks at camera often (it's her real audience)
  • Smiles easily, even when confused
  • Sits forward, engaged posture
  • Hair/makeup done for camera — she came prepared
Wardrobe
  • Put together but "relatable" — not too fancy
  • Something that reads well on camera
  • Maybe a signature accessory (the scarf she mentions)
  • Bright or warm colors — she wants to pop on screen
Key Moments to Nail
"I KNEW it! Disconnect to reconnect!"
She's genuinely excited, not sarcastic.
"When I went to Bali..."
She connects his answer to her own experience immediately. Automatic.
"Or a feeling?"
Her proudest moment. She thinks she's finally matching his depth.
"He didn't even plug his blog."
Genuine confusion. In her world, this is the whole point.
The empty chair moment
She recovers fast. The show must go on.
Reference Points
  • Influencers who interview people but make it about themselves
  • The friend who live-tweets their own experiences
  • Someone who says "I'm such an empath" but doesn't notice when someone's struggling
  • Podcast hosts who use phrases like "unpack that" and "let's get vulnerable"
MR. TRAVELLER
The Passenger
"A man who has seen too much and learned to say too little."
He travels between worlds. Not metaphorically. Literally. He puts on sunglasses and enters different realities. A phone rings and pulls him back to his anchor point — a yellow payphone on a concrete wall.
He doesn't know why this happens. He doesn't know how long it's been happening. He's stopped asking. Now he just... moves through.
The podcast studio is just another world he's passing through. The host is just another person who won't understand. He's not bitter about this. He's just tired. And patient. And surprisingly at peace.
Energy
  • Still. Very still.
  • Economy of movement — doesn't fidget, doesn't fill space
  • When he moves, it's deliberate
  • Calm surface, but there's depth underneath
  • Not cold — warm, actually. Just contained.
Speech Patterns
  • Short answers. Not rude, just efficient.
  • Doesn't use filler words ("um," "like," "you know")
  • Pauses before answering — actually considers the question
  • Speaks in statements, not questions
  • When he says something profound, he doesn't emphasize it — it just lands
  • Comfortable with silence
  • SHRUGS instead of saying "maybe" or "sure"
What He Wants
  • To get through this without incident
  • For the ringing to not start yet
  • Maybe, somewhere deep — to be understood (but he's given up on that)
  • To find his way back. Always.
What He's Hiding
  • The tinnitus/ringing that's building
  • That this studio is just one of many worlds he's been in today
  • That everything he says about "travel" is literally true
  • That he's genuinely enjoying parts of this conversation, despite himself
Physical Notes
  • Sunglasses on throughout the interview
  • Touches his ear when the ringing starts (subtle, not dramatic)
  • Shrugs instead of verbal fillers
  • Small smiles instead of laughing
  • Leans back — comfortable, not defensive
  • When he stands to leave, it's sudden but not panicked
Wardrobe
  • Simple. Practical. Clothes you can move in.
  • Nothing that screams "look at me"
  • Muted colors — he's used to blending in
  • The sunglasses are the only statement piece
  • Leather jacket. Dark t-shirt. Dark pants.
  • Slightly rumpled — he's been places today
The Tinnitus/Ringing — Actor Notes
  • First hit: Faint. Like a distant phone. He notices, touches his ear, moves on. He's experienced enough to manage it.
  • Second hit: Stronger. He closes his eyes briefly. Has to refocus. The host notices something's off.
  • Third hit (crescendo): Loud. Unavoidable. He knows he has to go NOW. This is when he stands.

It's not pain. It's a pull. Like something tugging at his chest. The sound isn't unpleasant — it's insistent. It's the phone calling him back.

Key Moments to Nail
"I always travel with them."
Simple truth. No mystery intended.
First tinnitus hit
Don't oversell it. Just a pause. A touch of the ear. Then back.
"How can you be found without being lost first?"
He's not trying to be profound. It's just obvious to him.
The dog story
This is his warmth showing. He finds it funny. Genuine affection.
"Late. Lucky. Leaving."
Three truths. He's always late. He's lucky (he finds his way back). He's leaving (always).
"Thank you for having me."
Genuine. He means it.
Reference Points
  • Someone who's been in a war but doesn't talk about it
  • A traveler who's seen so much that nothing surprises them anymore
  • Calm, kind, but clearly carrying something unseen
  • A person at a party who's genuinely listening while everyone else performs
THE DYNAMIC BETWEEN THEM
She talks. He listens.
She fills space. He lets space exist.
She performs connection. He accidentally achieves it.
She thinks she's interviewing him. He's just passing through.

The comedy comes from her misinterpreting everything he says. He talks about literal world-jumping; she hears "travel blogger with commitment issues." He talks about being pulled back by a ringing phone; she hears "work-life balance struggles."

And he lets her. Because explaining is pointless. And because she's not wrong, in her own way.

Visual

The opening is 5 SECONDS. Ring. Wall. Sunglasses. ZAAP. Studio. That's it. Don't linger. The speed is the point. The audience should go "wait, what was that?" — and then they're in the podcast before they can process it.

// The Loop

SHORT FILM & ENDING

LOOP 01
Wrong World
Learn the mechanics. Show he can't escape on his own. Establish the ring as his exit.
LOOP 02
The Podcast
THE REVELATION — this is the interview we just watched. We see him enter the studio. We see him get pulled back. Audience realizes: "Holy shit, the podcast was one of his worlds."
LOOP 03
Escalation (Optional)
Things get more intense/strange. Ring comes faster. Sets up the ending.
THE SMIRK
The Final Image
Back at the wall. Receiver to ear. Now the sound is CLEAR — the theme song. He listens. Understanding washes over him. He looks at the camera. First time. Only time. He smirks. "Welcome to the show." Theme song kicks in FULL. Black.

"Tired of the pressure? Stop knowing the time. The Chron-NO. Ignorance is luxury."
