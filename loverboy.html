Episode 02 • Production Bible v1.0

LOVER-BOY

A man obsessed with commitment sits for a podcast interview. Then we see where his answers come from.

HOLD. WALK. NEVER LET GO.

Enter The Loop
// Overview

THE CONCEPT

LOGLINE

A man who is obsessively, almost supernaturally committed to love appears as a guest on a comedy podcast. The host treats him like a curiosity. Then the camera follows him out of the studio — into a misty forest, a decaying cabin, and an eternal loop he cannot escape.

THEME

Commitment as a loop. What happens when you never let go? When you keep walking, keep holding hands, keep going forward — and discover that "forever" isn't a destination but a prison you built yourself?

// Visual Mechanics
01
The Walk
Two figures walking, holding hands, through a misty forest. This is the visual spine of the film. The walk IS the loop.
02
The Keyring
A small keyring shaped like a cabin. Found on the ground. Triggers curiosity — the one thing Loverboy fears. Chekhov's gun.
03
The Cabin
Same shape as the keyring. Inside: a decayed version of himself, watching the podcast on a TV that won't turn off.
04
The TV
Plays the actual podcast audio from Part 1. Every channel. The same interview. His own voice explaining the rules of his own prison.
// Chekhov's Guns
Cold Open
Whispered "Don't let go" / "Never". Reappears as the literal moment their hands separate at the cabin.
"I don't remember yesterday"
Said in the podcast. Repeated in the forest. It's not philosophy — it's literal amnesia from the loop.
The Keyring
Found. Taken by Blondie. Matches the cabin. The cabin dissolves back into it. Loop reset.
"Your hands are freezing"
Said in the cold open. Repeated as the final line. The loop completes.
// Characters

THE CAST

THE HOST
Podcast Host • The Normal One
"My commitment grows only towards cancelling plans."
The host of Nod Another Podcast. Energetic, self-deprecating, and genuinely curious — but completely out of his depth with this guest. He treats Loverboy like a quirky relationship guru when what he's actually interviewing is a man trapped in an eternal loop. The comedy comes from his misreadings. The pathos comes from the one moment he stops performing and actually feels something.
Performance Notes
  • Big energy in the opening — classic podcast mode
  • Gradually quieter as the interview deepens
  • The punchlines land because he's genuinely trying to keep things light
  • By 'weakness & endings' he's stopped performing entirely
  • Post-credits: alone in the studio. The first time we see him without words.
Key Lines
  • "Entertainment is optional but nodding is compulsory"
  • "My commitment grows only towards cancelling plans"
  • "I don't think I can make fun of that"
  • "So that happened. Toh kal milte hain?"
LOVER-BOY
The Guest • The Committed One
"Never let go and just continue."
A man who is commitment incarnate. Still, calm, slightly distant — he doesn't perform his presence, he just is. Every answer is simple, direct, and slightly terrifying. He speaks about commitment the way someone speaks about breathing. He walks through a forest holding her hand because stopping is unimaginable. He doesn't remember yesterday because the loop erases everything except the present step. He is not tragic. He is committed. And that's what makes it tragic.
Performance Notes
  • Never rushes. Let silences hang.
  • Minimal physical movement. Still hands. Small nods.
  • When he says something profound, he doesn't know it's profound.
  • The longest pause in the entire episode comes before the word 'Once.'
  • In the forest: same clothes as the podcast. Continuity.
Key Lines
  • "Entertainment is a byproduct. Just like happiness."
  • "You need space to forget. And I don't want to forget."
  • "I don't remember yesterday."
  • "This moment. This one. This is all there is. Forever is right now."
  • "Curiosity. It makes you look back. That's how you stop."
  • "Once."
BLONDIE
Her • The One He Walks With
"God, your hands are freezing."
She is only in the film section. We never learn her name — she's "her." Black dress. She walks beside him through the forest, holding his hand. She is warm where he is still. Playful where he is measured. She takes the keyring from his hand like a child grabbing something shiny — spontaneous, alive. She doesn't want him to enter the cabin. She pulls his arm gently. She knows something he doesn't — or maybe she just knows that what's inside is worse than what's outside.
Performance Notes
  • Physical warmth: lean into him, bump his shoulder
  • Playful energy when she takes the keyring — NOT possessive
  • Unease when the cabin appears — subtle, not horror-movie
  • Her "Don't—" when he lets go is the emotional climax of the film
  • She is the loop. He is the one who might break it.
Key Lines
  • "I don't even remember what we were fighting about."
  • "As long as you're holding my hand, I don't mind."
  • "Let's just keep walking."
  • "Don't—"
  • "God, your hands are freezing." (final line / loop reset)
DECAYED LOVERBOY
The One In The Chair • What Happens If You Stop
He never speaks. He never turns around. He is enough.
Same clothes as Loverboy, heavily distressed. Grey powder on skin. Sitting in a chair inside the cabin, watching the podcast on a TV that won't turn off. He hasn't moved in decades. We never fully see his face. We don't need to. The shape of his body in the chair, lit by the TV, is enough. What destroys the audience is that current Loverboy is looking at the back of his own head. This is what "stopping" looks like.
Production Notes
  • Same clothes as Loverboy, heavily distressed
  • Grey powder on skin — tired, not zombified
  • He's a man who sat in a chair for decades, not a horror monster
  • The horror is in the stillness, not the makeup
  • Shoot the chair (decayed) first, then walking (current) second
  • One small adjustment — the way someone who hasn't moved in years reminds the world they still exist
THE DYNAMIC
The Host talks. Loverboy listens.
The Host fills space. Loverboy lets space exist.
The Host performs connection. Loverboy accidentally achieves it.
The Host thinks he's interviewing someone unusual. He has no idea he's interviewing someone trapped.

The comedy comes from the host misinterpreting everything Loverboy says. Loverboy talks about an eternal loop; the host hears "really devoted boyfriend." Loverboy talks about never remembering yesterday; the host hears "lives in the moment." Every profound truth sounds like a cute relationship take — until it doesn't.

// Visual Screenplay

THE SCRIPT

RUNTIME: ~13 MINUTES • PODCAST + SHORT FILM
Part 1 — The Podcast
Scene 01
COLD OPEN
0:00–0:15
Before the intro. Black screen. Forest sounds — wind, distant birds, footsteps on leaves.
BLONDIE (V.O.)
(whisper)
Don't let go.
LOVERBOY (V.O.)
(whisper)
Never.
Beat. Sound cuts. Studio lights snap on.
The audience doesn't know what this is yet. It's a seed. They'll understand at the end.
Scene 02
INTRO
0:15–1:45
HOST
Welcome welcome to Nod Another Podcast, where entertainment is optional but nodding is compulsory.
HOST
Actually the makers of Nod Another Podcast are big commit-o-phobes. I mean that they can't commit to anything. They could not even commit to a fixed format. Even they don't know ki 5 minute baad kya hone lagega.
HOST
So we decided to start our show with someone who could teach us a few things about commitment. Please welcome the one and only… Lover-Boy.
LOVERBOY enters. Still, calm, slightly distant. He doesn't perform his entrance.
HOST
Welcome, man. Thanks for coming.
LOVERBOY
(small nod)
…
HOST
…Okay. Not a words guy. That's fine. Let's get into it.
LOVERBOY
(after a beat)
Is entertainment really the purpose?
HOST
…What?
LOVERBOY
Entertainment is a byproduct. Just like happiness is a byproduct of things you do. Aap khush thodi na karte ho. Aap kuch karte ho jisse aapko khushi milti hai.
HOST
(to camera)
He's been here 30 seconds and I'm already being educated. So gyaan ki baatein toh hoti rahengi.
Scene 03
1) COMMITMENT
1:30–3:00
HOST
So Lover-Boy… commitment. What does commitment mean to you?
LOVERBOY
Never let go and just continue.
HOST
Okay, good start— but continue what?
LOVERBOY
Continue whatever you started.
HOST
Like a job? A relationship? A Netflix series?
LOVERBOY
(doesn't acknowledge the joke)
No, I mean—never let go. Always be there by their side.
HOST
Always?
LOVERBOY
Yeah. This is how commitment works.
HOST
But what about privacy?
LOVERBOY
Privacy is for people who aren't actually committed. Or don't understand commitment.
HOST
(light, not insulting)
Okay… that's a scary sentence for someone like me but said very politely.
HOST
Do you ever take a break? Like, go for a solo trip, see a movie alone?
LOVERBOY
Why would I?
HOST
Because… you're a person? With your own thoughts?
LOVERBOY
Thoughts are fine. But they happen alongside. You don't need space to think. You need space to forget. And I don't want to forget.
This line should hang in the air. The host doesn't fully understand it. The audience might not either — yet.
HOST
…Okay. Respect.
Scene 04
2) PEOPLE CHANGE
3:00–4:15
HOST
But what if the person changes?
LOVERBOY
If you are truly committed then they'll never change.
HOST
Never? Like, day one energy forever?
LOVERBOY
Your commitment will be the same on the first day and the 1000th day. It can grow stronger, not weaker.
HOST
But from personal experience people do change?
LOVERBOY
Then they were never truly committed.
HOST
What do you mean that people never change?
LOVERBOY
People evolve and grow but something in them always remains the same. And you commit to that.
HOST
Hmm. My commitment grows only towards cancelling plans.
LOVERBOY
That's also a kind of commitment.
HOST
(accepts the L)
Okay. Respect. Let's move on.
Scene 05
3) HOBBIES
4:15–5:00
HOST
Tell us about your hobbies.
LOVERBOY
We walk together.
HOST
Okay… anything else?
LOVERBOY
Sometimes we walk faster.
HOST
(waits for more — nothing comes)
Sports? Movies? Food?
LOVERBOY
Walking is the ultimate sport. Every step is a love letter to the earth.
HOST
Wow that's almost poetic.
LOVERBOY
(shrugs)
…
HOST
Where do you walk? Like, park? Beach? Mountains?
LOVERBOY
(considers — slightly longer pause)
Trees. Nature.
HOST
With?
LOVERBOY
(pause)
Her.
HOST
Her? Who's her?
LOVERBOY
We walk together. That's all.
The host notices 'her' but Loverboy shuts it down. Don't push further — save the mystery.
Scene 06
4) HOME
5:00–5:30
HOST
Speaking of — where is home for you?
LOVERBOY
(long pause — actually seems to not know)
…
HOST
You don't know?
LOVERBOY
It's hard to explain.
HOST
Try.
LOVERBOY
(quiet)
Wherever we stop.
HOST
And where's that?
LOVERBOY
We haven't stopped yet.
Scene 07
5) YESTERDAY
5:45–7:15
HOST
This man is so obsessed with commitment that nothing else seems to matter to him. I have not met such a focused human being ever. If I ask you what you'll do tomorrow, you'll say walking—hands in hand. Walk us through… let's say yesterday. What did you do yesterday?
LOVERBOY
I don't remember yesterday.
HOST
(smirks)
BUT, yesterday was just yesterday. If you don't wanna tell us then it's okay. Then tell us about tomorrow.
LOVERBOY
No, you don't get it. Nothing matters. Not your name. Not my name. Not what I did yesterday. Or what you will do tomorrow.
HOST
(genuine pause)
…Okay that hit different.
LOVERBOY
(doesn't acknowledge the host's reaction)
This moment. This one. This is all there is. Forever isn't some distant thing waiting to happen. Forever is right now. This is forever. Right here. And when this moment dies… the next one begins. Another forever.
Hold on Loverboy's face. He's not performing. He's describing his literal reality: an eternal loop. The host is moved but doesn't understand the literal truth.
HOST
That's… I honestly don't know what to say. Most people on this show say something absurd and I make fun of it. I don't think I can make fun of that.
LOVERBOY
(small nod)
That's also okay.
Scene 08
6) WEAKNESS & ENDINGS
7:15–8:45
HOST
Committed logon ki weakness kya hoti hai?
LOVERBOY
Endings.
HOST
Endings?
LOVERBOY
If something ends… it gets left behind. And then you're…
(trails off — doesn't finish the sentence)
HOST
(fills the silence)
Same. That's why my episodes are tiny. Main ending se pehle hi end kar deta hoon.
LOVERBOY
(nods — barely)
…
HOST
So you avoid endings?
LOVERBOY
I keep going.
HOST
And your personal weakness? Like, specifically yours?
LOVERBOY
Curiosity.
HOST
How is curiosity a weakness?
LOVERBOY
When you start using your brain and pollute the moment with any thought… you stop being. Curiosity makes you ask 'why.' It makes you look back. That's how you stop.
HOST
So what do you do?
LOVERBOY
I keep going. Forward.
HOST
Have you ever… not kept going?
This is the question. The one the host asks almost accidentally. The room shifts. Something in Loverboy's body changes.
LOVERBOY
(long pause — the longest in the entire interview; quietly)
Once.
Scene 09
THE TRANSITION
8:45–9:15
Silence. The host doesn't know how to follow that.
HOST
(softly)
What happened?
Loverboy looks at his hands. Cold. He closes them into fists, then opens them.
LOVERBOY
I should go.
HOST
What? We still have—
LOVERBOY
(stands — not abruptly, but with certainty)
She's waiting.
HOST
She? Who's—
Loverboy walks toward the edge of the set.
As he reaches the threshold — the studio falls away. The lights soften. The walls dissolve into fog. The sound of the studio gives way to wind, leaves, distant cold. We hear the cold open again — but now it's not a memory. It's happening.
BLONDIE
God, your hands are freezing.
Loverboy steps through. He's in the forest. Her hand reaches for his. He takes it. They walk.
Part 2 — The Film
Scene 10
BEAT 1 — THE WALK
9:15–9:50
Wide shot. Cold, misty forest. Two figures walking, holding hands.
HER
I don't even remember what we were fighting about.
HIM
Neither do I.
HER
It felt important at the time.
HIM
Everything does.
HER
How long ago was that?
He squeezes her hand instead of answering.
HIM
We're almost home.
HER
You said that yesterday.
HIM
Did I? I don't remember yesterday.
HER
Doesn't matter.
Scene 11
BEAT 2 — THE KEYRING
9:50–10:20
He notices something on the ground. Slows.
HIM
(bending to pick it up)
Hm. Interesting.
A small keyring — clearly shaped like a cabin. She reaches forward and takes it from his hand — playful, spontaneous, like a child grabbing something shiny.
She should almost bump his shoulder after taking it. Light, warm, alive. NOT possessive.
His face: the briefest flicker. Not anger — a hint of interrupted curiosity. Gone in half a second. He reaches for her hand again. They walk.
Scene 12
BEAT 3 — THE LOOP RESUMES
10:20–10:45
HER
I feel like we've been walking forever.
HIM
Maybe we have.
HER
As long as you're holding my hand, I don't mind.
He scans the treeline. She doesn't notice. His hand is a little looser in hers.
Scene 13
BEAT 4 — THE CABIN
10:45–11:15
Through the fog: a structure. A small wooden cabin. Same shape as the keyring. He recognizes it. Stops.
HER
Let's just keep walking.
HIM
(staring)
Wait.
He holds the keyring up. The shapes match.
HER
(pulling his arm gently)
Come on.
HIM
(doesn't move)
I want to have a look.
HER
How will you find anything in there?
HIM
It'll find me. I'll be back.
He slides his hand from hers. Slow. Deliberate. Close-up on their fingers pulling apart.
HER
(quietly)
Don't—
He's already walking toward the cabin. She stands on the path. Alone.
Scene 14
BEAT 5 — INSIDE THE CABIN
11:15–12:15
He enters. Dim interior. Dusty. Small. One harsh light source.
A figure. Sitting in a chair, back partially turned. Same build, same clothes — but WASHED OUT. Everything about him is faded. He's been sitting here for decades.
THE TV: An old CRT TV alive with static. On screen: the NodAnotherPodcast interview. The one we just watched.
TV HOST
"So Lover-Boy… commitment. What does commitment mean to you?"
TV LOVERBOY
"Never let go and just continue."
He changes the channel.
TV HOST
"What did you do yesterday?"
TV LOVERBOY
"I don't remember yesterday."
Changes again.
TV LOVERBOY
"Curiosity. When you start using your brain and pollute the moment with any thought… you stop being."
Changes again.
TV HOST
"Have you ever… not kept going?"
TV LOVERBOY
"…Once."
Every channel. The same interview. He switches the TV off. Beat. It clicks back on by itself.
TV LOVERBOY
"I keep going. Forward."
The decayed Loverboy never turns around. We never fully see his face. The shape of his body in the chair, lit by the TV, is enough. What destroys the audience is that current Loverboy is looking at the back of his own head.
Current Loverboy steps backward. Reaches for the door.
Scene 15
BEAT 6 — THE DISSOLVE
12:15–12:35
The walls soften. Edges lose definition. Colors bleed. The TV image warps. The cabin folds. Collapses. Shrinks. Compresses into a small, dense object.
A keyring. In the shape of a cabin. On the forest floor.
Scene 16
BEAT 7 — THE LOOP
12:35–12:50
Wide shot. Fog. Forest. Two figures walking, holding hands.
HIM
Hm. Interesting.
She reaches over. Playfully takes it from his hand.
HER
God, your hands are freezing.
CUT TO BLACK
Scene 17
POST-CREDITS BEAT
12:50–13:00
Black screen. The podcast studio. The host sits alone. Mic on. Guest chair empty.
HOST
(looking at the empty chair, then at camera)
…So that happened.
(beat)
HOST
Toh kal milte hain?
The host doesn't know what happened. He doesn't understand where the guest went. But something in his face says he felt it. It's the first time the host is quiet — and that's the punchline.
END.

TOTAL~13 minutes

The Transition is the spine of this episode. The studio should feel like it wasn't real — like the podcast was happening inside the loop all along.

No VFX transition needed. Studio lights dimming + fog at edges + sound design shift (studio hum → wind) is enough. The CUT from studio to forest should be hard and clean.

What Plays On The TV: Use the actual podcast audio from Part 1. Same recording.

Record the podcast first → Extract 4 key audio clips (commitment, yesterday, curiosity, "once") → Play them on the TV during the cabin shoot → The actor reacts to his own voice. This creates visceral recursion.
